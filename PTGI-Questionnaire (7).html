Self-Report Measure

Posttraumatic Growth Inventory

Dear participant,

You are about to answer a short questionnaire about changes you may have noticed in yourself since going through a difficult or painful experience. This is not a test, and there are no right or wrong answers. Growth and pain often live side by side — noticing one does not erase the other. Please answer honestly, according to how things truly are for you, and we will look at the results together.

— Glenn Cohen, Clinical Psychologist
Trauma Specialist

Instructions: Please think of the crisis or difficult event you have experienced. For each of the statements below, indicate the degree to which this change occurred in your life as a result of your crisis, using the following scale:

0 I did not experience this change as a result of my crisis. 1 I experienced this change to a very small degree as a result of my crisis. 2 I experienced this change to a small degree as a result of my crisis. 3 I experienced this change to a moderate degree as a result of my crisis. 4 I experienced this change to a great degree as a result of my crisis. 5 I experienced this change to a very great degree as a result of my crisis.

There are no right or wrong answers. The questionnaire has 21 statements and takes about 5–10 minutes.
